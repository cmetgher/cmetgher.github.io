As part of the PhD student rotation at the Computational Social Science department I had the opportunity to work closely with a wide range on investigators on several research projects. I thoroughly enjoy transdiciplinary research and am deeply appreciative of working in a research area where transdiciplinary work is the norm.

Research

I am interested in the social determinants of cancer and looking at how these factors interact at multiple levels across time to influence the dynamics of cancer. Social factors matter - as they were shown to influence vulnerability to disease, risk-taking behaviors, availability (and quality) of health care and contribute to a better understanding of societal patterns of cancer. They have often been referred to as the “the fundamental cause of health and disease” and “the causes of causes”.

I use agent-based computational modeling for approaching the complexity inherent in the social determinants of cancer. This partly implies creating artificial societies of heterogeneous agents that interact between themselves and with the environment, contacting disease as they age. My work is informed by research across several disciplines and relevant publicly available datasets on cancer incidence and mortality.

Other side projects I worked on with collaborators from other disciplines involved modeling suicide as a complex system, modeling marriage squeeze, building a web based framwork for ABM, and others.

Resources

Some tools for building ABMs include: Repast Suite, MASON, NetLogo, Swarm, etc. For statistical computing I prefer: R and R Studio. My favourite productivity tool is: Pomodoro.

Tutorials: If you’re new to ABM I’d recommend starting from this On-Line Guide for Newcomers to Agent-Based Modeling. It has introductory readings and supporting materials to help newcomers get to know ABM. The Java tutorials from Oracle are another great resource to work on you programming skills. After you’re done with that, you might want to give these two tools a try: MASON tutorials, Repast Symphony tutorials. Netlogo tutorials are also a great resource if you have less experience with software programming.
If you get stuck in R, you might find some answers here: Tutorials on how to do stuff in R in 2 minutes or less
