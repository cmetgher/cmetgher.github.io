Agent based modeling (ABM)

ABM is particularly suited to address the complexity inherent in the social determinants of cancer by allowing to incorporate heterogeneity, 'local' interaction, and bounded rationality while focusing on dynamics (that can lead to equilibrium or non-equilibrium processes).

Social network analysis (SNA)

In the context of social determinants of cancer, SNA provides means to make sense of the complex set of relations between system components at all levels, from individuals to society.

Social determinants of cancer

Social determinants refer to the conditions in which people are born, grow, live, work, age and die. These factors interact dynamically across time to affect the dynamics of cancer by influencing and shaping individual and population health behaviours.
